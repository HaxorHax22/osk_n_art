My Artistic Journey

Born and raised in Reykjavík, I've been surrounded by Iceland's dramatic natural beauty my entire life. The way morning light transforms volcanic rock, how glaciers hold infinite shades of blue and gray, the ethereal dance of aurora across winter skies—these experiences shaped my understanding of color, texture, and emotion.

After studying at the Iceland Academy of the Arts, I discovered that abstract expression allowed me to capture not just what I see, but what I feel. Each piece is an emotional translation of Iceland's raw energy—the kind that can't be captured in photographs but lives in memory and sensation.

"I don't paint Iceland's landscapes—I paint how Iceland feels."

My Creative Process

Inspiration

Each piece begins with a memory or feeling from Iceland's landscapes—the weight of ancient ice, the warmth of volcanic stone.

Creation

Using high-quality acrylics and mixed media, I build layers intuitively, letting each mark inform the next.

Evolution

Pieces develop over weeks or months, with rest periods that allow me to see with fresh eyes and make decisive choices.
